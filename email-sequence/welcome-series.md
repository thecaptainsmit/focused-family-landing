## Email 2: Quick Win (Day 1)
**Subject:** The 2-word phrase that changed our mornings
**Preview:** "First shoes, then park" — why this works when everything else fails

---

Hi [Name],

Quick question:

Does your child melt down when it's time to leave the playground? Get dressed? Stop playing?

Try this today:

**"First [thing they don't want], then [thing they want]"**

Examples:
- "First shoes, then park"
- "First brush teeth, then story"
- "First homework, then screen time"

**Why this works (the science):**

ADHD brains struggle with working memory. When you say "time to leave," they literally can't hold "park ending" and "next activity" in their head at the same time. It creates panic.

"First-Then" externalizes this. They can SEE the sequence. The "then" becomes a dopamine target — something to work toward.

**The research:** Visual schedules with immediate rewards reduce transition meltdowns by up to 70% in kids with ADHD (Van der Heijden et al., Sleep Medicine Reviews, 2022).

**How to use it:**
1. Get a piece of paper
2. Draw a line down the middle
3. Left side: "FIRST" + picture of task
4. Right side: "THEN" + picture of reward
5. Point to it. Don't explain. Don't negotiate.

Try it 3 times today. Consistency is everything.
